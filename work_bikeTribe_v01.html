Bike Tribe

Description
Bike Tribe is an application for smartphones that connects cyclists in need of mechanical assistance with a supportive community of fellow riders who are willing to help.
Whether it's a flat tire, a broken chain, or any other bike-related issue, Bike Tribe enables users to quickly find nearby cyclists who are skilled in bike repairs and willing to lend a hand. This application aims to solve this commonly encountered problem by many cyclists by offering a practical solution to get help when they encounter minor mechanical issues with their bicycles.
By facilitating the connection between cyclists in need of assistance and those willing to help, the app overcomes the obstacles related to repairing the small mechanical hitches encountered daily. This fosters a smoother and more enjoyable cycling experience, strengthening the cycling community around mutual aid and skill-sharing.

Application functions

  • Assistance Requests: Users can submit assistance requests (by describing their specific mechanical problem, A chat feature allows users to communicate directly with cyclist mechanics to discuss the problem and assistance details, or premade problem description). Real-Time Notifications: Users receive real-time notifications when a nearby cyclist offers their help.
  • Bike shop Location nearby: The app identifies nearby bike shops who can provide assistance in case of a mechanical issue.
  • A point system is awarded when a cyclist helps repair another cyclist's bike issue. These points can then be used as a voucher for purchases at partner bike shops. Ratings and Reviews: Users can leave ratings and reviews on cyclist mechanics to share their experiences with the community.
  • Maintenance tips, video tutorials, and recommendations for bike shops can also be included to enhance the user experience.

Target groups
The users of the app will primarily be cyclists who encounter mechanical issues with their bikes and seek assistance. They may include both experienced cyclists and beginners who may not have the necessary knowledge or tools to fix their bikes themselves. Additionally, the app will attract cyclist mechanics who are willing to offer their help and expertise to others in need. The users may vary in age, skill level, and cycling preferences, but they share a common interest in cycling and a desire to build a supportive community around bike maintenance and repair. The audience of this app can be defined as follows:

  • The main target is the urban citizen, particularly in areas with well-developed cycling networks. They have an urban lifestyle and seek a functional solution. Their motivation is to take care of their health and have an easy and convenient means of transportation.
  • People who wish to socialize. They have a social profile, and their motivation is to meet and connect with others.
  • Individuals who enjoy sharing their knowledge or helping others. They have a "teacher-like" profile and take pleasure in sharing their expertise. Feeling useful to others is their source of motivation.
  • Individuals seeking discounts on bicycle-related equipment. This group has an economic profile, and their motivation is to save money.
  • People who cycle for its low emissions. This group has a more political profile as they want to align their daily actions with their environmental beliefs. Their motivation revolves around ecological considerations.
  • Experienced cyclists who want to promote cycling by making it easier for beginners. They have an altruistic profile, as cycling is a strong passion for them, and they aim to spread it to as many people as possible.

Client/ Designer goals
What are my goals? On wich aspect (political, social, financal or functional) my app should emphasize?

Politic aspect: 20% Financal aspect: 25% Functional aspect: 20% Social aspect: 35%

With a higher focus on the social aspect, the application is more likely to attract a larger user base, leading to increased community engagement and potential revenue generation. This, in turn, enables more efficient development of new features and provides greater weight to the application's political goals related to reducing greenhouse gas emissions. I believe than a balanced approach that highlights the social aspect while maintaining functionality can lead to a successful and sustainable app.
